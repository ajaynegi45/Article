Great topic! Here's an outline and some tips to help you write a compelling and informative article on how ancient Indian Vedas, scientific ideas, and knowledge can be applied to today's computer science technology:

I. Introduction

Briefly explain the topic and why it's relevant to computer science students
Introduce the idea that ancient Indian knowledge can be applied to modern technology
II. Ancient Indian Science and Technology

Provide an overview of ancient Indian science and technology, including achievements in mathematics, astronomy, medicine, and philosophy
Discuss the importance of the Vedas and other ancient texts in preserving this knowledge
III. Connections to Modern Computer Science

Identify specific examples of how ancient Indian knowledge can be applied to modern computer science technology, such as algorithms, data structures, and artificial intelligence
Explain how these concepts relate to ancient Indian ideas, such as the concept of "maya" and the importance of intuition in problem-solving
IV. Case Studies and Examples

Provide case studies or examples of how ancient Indian knowledge has been applied in modern technology, such as in the development of Google's PageRank algorithm or IBM's Watson computer system
Discuss the benefits and limitations of using ancient knowledge in modern technology
V. Conclusion

Summarize the key points of the article and reiterate the relevance of ancient Indian knowledge to modern computer science technology
Provide some suggestions for how computer science students can further explore these concepts and incorporate them into their studies and careers.
Tips for Writing:

Research and cite reliable sources to support your claims and provide evidence for your arguments
Use clear and concise language, avoiding technical jargon unless necessary
Provide examples and anecdotes to help illustrate your points and make the content more engaging
Make sure the article is well-organized and flows logically from one section to the next
Keep in mind your audience and their level of familiarity with the subject matter, providing enough background information without overwhelming them with details.
